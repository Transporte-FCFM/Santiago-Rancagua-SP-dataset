# Stated Preference Survey on Interurban Mode Choice
## Santiago–Rancagua Corridor, Chile
This  dataset contains the responses to a Stated Preference (SP) discrete choice experiment on interurban public transport mode choice in the Santiago-Rancagua corridor (central Chile, aprox. 100 km), complemented with sociodemographics, travel patterns, a 20-item psychometric battery, and stated satisfaction with the habitual service of each mode.

# Data Dictionary

**File:** `sp_santiago_rancagua_public.csv`
**Observations:** 5,460 rows (420 respondents × 13 choice tasks)
**Fieldwork:** May 2026, single wave, online panel.
**Funding:** [Daziano Research Group](https://daziano.cee.cornell.edu/), [School of Civil and Environmental Engineering](https://www.duffield.cornell.edu/cee), Cornell University, USA; FONDECYT Regular 1231584 (ANID, Chile).

## 1. Overview and Structure

The dataset contains the responses to a Stated Preference (SP) discrete choice experiment on interurban public transport mode choice in the Santiago–Rancagua corridor (central Chile), complemented with sociodemographics, travel patterns, a 20-item psychometric battery, and stated satisfaction with the habitual service of each mode.

### 1.1 Study corridor

The corridor connects Santiago (pop. 7.4M), the national capital, with Rancagua (pop. 274k), a regional capital, spanning approximately 100 km and including several intermediate towns from which trips originate towards the larger cities, the main hubs for work and study.

Each respondent reported the destination of their most recent trip (with origin "Santiago") in the corridor and was assigned the SP design corresponding to that OD pair. Designs were generated by pivoting attribute levels around average travel times and costs observed for each OD pair and mode combination, so that the hypothetical scenarios remained realistic for the respondent's actual trip context.

### 1.2 Structure

The dataset is in *long format*: each row is one *stated-choice task* completed by a respondent. There are 13 rows per respondent. Sociodemographic variables, travel patterns, psychometric indicators, and satisfaction items are constant across the 13 rows of a respondent; SP attributes vary by row.

| Concept | Value |
|---|---|
| Rows per respondent | 13 |
| Tasks 1–12 | D-efficient design tasks |
| Task 13 | Dominated task (attention check) |
| Alternatives | Train (commuter rail), Bus (intercity coach), Micro (rural bus, where available), Opt-out |

**Mode naming convention (used as column prefixes):**

| Prefix | Mode | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `train` | Commuter rail | EFE Alameda–Rancagua intercity commuter rail |
| `bus` | Intercity coach bus | e.g. FlixBus, other single and double-decker coach buses |
| `micro` | Rural bus | Small rural buses |

**Quality screening applied to this file:**

1. Responses flagged as low quality at ingestion were removed.
2. Respondents spending < 30 s on the 20-item psychometric page (straight-lining) were removed.

## 3. Sociodemographics

Constant within respondent. Provided by the Netquest panel or answered directly in the survey.

| Field | Type | Coding | Question |
|---|---|---|---|
| `age` | Integer | ≥ 18, years | "Please indicate your age." |
| `gender` | Categorical | `1` = Male, `2` = Female, `3` = Other/Prefer not to say (not present in sample) | "Which gender do you identify with?" |
| `income_gse` | Ordinal | `1` = AB (highest) … `7` = E (lowest); Chilean GSE household socioeconomic classification (see table) | "Please indicate your total household income." |
| `household_size` | Integer | ≥ 1, persons | "Please indicate the number of persons in your household." |
| `has_licence` | Binary | `1` = Yes, `2` = No | "Do you hold a driving licence (class B) or a professional licence?" |
| `n_cars_household` | Integer | `0` = no cars, `1`, `2`, `3`, `4` = four or more cars | "How many cars are there in your household?" |
| `reduced_fare` | Binary | `0` = Standard fare, `1` = Reduced fare | Whether the respondent holds a reduced-fare benefit (student TNE or senior). Determines which cost column was displayed in the SP tasks. |

**GSE socioeconomic groups (Chile):**

|---|---|---|

| 3 | C1b | Upper-middle |
| 4 | C2 | Middle |

| 6 | D | Low |

Constant within respondent. Refer to the respondent's most recent/habitual trip in the corridor (departing from Santiago).

| Field | Type | Coding | Question |
|---|---|---|---|
| `destination` | Categorical | See table below | "In which of the following localities did the last trip you recall taking (departing from Santiago) in the Santiago–Rancagua corridor end?" |
| `freq_use_train` | Ordinal | 1–6 frequency scale (below) | Frequency of use: intercity train (EFE Alameda–Rancagua) |
| `freq_use_bus` | Ordinal | 1–6 | Frequency of use: intercity coach bus |
| `freq_use_micro` | Ordinal | 1–6 | Frequency of use: rural bus |

## 5. Assigned Experimental Design

| Field | Type | Values | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| `od_design` | Text | `Buin-BuinZoo-Linderos`, `Paine`, `Hospital`, `San_Francisco`, `Graneros`, `Rancagua-Machali` | Origin–destination (OD) pair / D-efficient design assigned to the respondent, according to the OD pair the respondent reported. Determines available modes and attribute ranges. Attribute levels were pivoted around average travel times and costs per OD pair and mode. |
| `block` | Integer | 1–4 | Design block. Determines which of the 4 sets of 13 tasks was shown. |

**Rural bus availability:** the rural bus (`av_micro = 1`) is only available in designs `Buin-BuinZoo-Linderos` and `Paine`. In all other designs `av_micro = 0` and all `micro_*` columns are missing *by design* (structural missingness, not data loss).

## 6. Psychometric Indicators (`ind_01`–`ind_20`)

Battery of 20 attitudinal statements, *5-point Likert scale* (`1` = Strongly disagree … `5` = Strongly agree). Constant within respondent. Column `ind_NN` corresponds to indicator I*N* in the related publications. Statements were administered in Spanish; the English translations below are the reference wording.

**Introduction shown to respondents:** "Below we present a series of statements related to your experience, perceptions and emotions when using interurban public transport. Thinking of your habitual trips, please indicate your level of agreement or disagreement with each of them."

| Field | Statement |
|---|---|
| `ind_01` | "On the bus I can do things during the trip that I cannot do on the train (sleep, read, use my phone in peace)." |
| `ind_02` | "On the train I find it hard to concentrate or rest because of the noise and the people." |
| `ind_03` | "On the bus the environment is calmer and quieter than on the train." |
| `ind_04` | "I feel more in control when I can communicate with the driver or transport staff." |
| `ind_05` | "I value transport modes that reflect respect and dignity towards users." |
| `ind_06` | "Crowding is the factor that most affects my well-being during the trip, regardless of its duration." |
| `ind_07` | "When the vehicle is very full, I arrive at my destination noticeably more exhausted than if I had travelled with space." |
| `ind_08` | "The cleanliness and condition of the vehicle significantly influence how I feel during the trip." |
| `ind_09` | "When the vehicle has adequate climate control, the trip is noticeably more pleasant." |
| `ind_10` | "I prefer to arrive a few minutes later if it means travelling with greater comfort and space." |
| `ind_11` | "I enjoy the landscape when I travel; it helps me relax." |
| `ind_12` | "I consider the train to be the future of transport in this country." |
| `ind_13` | "I feel proud when I use modern and efficient public transport." |
| `ind_14` | "The bus and the rural bus are modes where I can have closer social interactions, compared to the train." |
| `ind_15` | "Investing in railway infrastructure is a concrete sign of the country's modernization and development." |
| `ind_16` | "The type of transport I habitually use reflects something about who I am and my values." |
| `ind_17` | "I am concerned that Chile continues to prioritize the car over quality interurban public transport." |
| `ind_18` | "I prefer more environmentally sustainable transport modes, even if they involve higher cost or travel time." |
| `ind_19` | "Quality public transport should be a guaranteed citizen right, not a privilege." |
| `ind_20` | "When I travel by train I feel I am using a mode of transport that represents progress." |

**Page timer:**

| Field | Description |
|---|---|

## 7. Satisfaction with Habitual Service, by Mode

Battery of 9 stated-satisfaction items about the respondent's habitual service on each mode. *Scale: 1–5* (`1` = Very dissatisfied … `5` = Very satisfied). Constant within respondent. `satis_micro_*` is structurally missing for respondents in OD pairs where the rural bus is not available.

**Introductory question (per mode):** "Regarding the [Intercity Train / Intercity Coach Bus / Interurban Rural Bus] alternative, how do you feel about the following aspects of the service?"

| Suffix `_N` | Item |
|---|---|
| 1 | "Service availability when I need it" |
| 2 | "Service operating hours" |
| 3 | "Available information (posted timetables, app, screens, WhatsApp channels, etc.)" |
| 4 | "Cost of the service" |
| 5 | "Personal safety on board during the trip" |
| 6 | "Ease of boarding and alighting" |
| 7 | "Comfort of on-board seats" |
| 8 | "Visual environment of the trip (landscape, lighting, visual cleanliness of the vehicle and surroundings)" |
| 9 | "Overall satisfaction with the service" |

Columns: `satis_train_1`–`satis_train_9`, `satis_bus_1`–`satis_bus_9`, `satis_micro_1`–`satis_micro_9`.

## 8. Choice Tasks and SP Experiment Attributes

### 8.1 Choice task variables (vary across rows)

| Field | Type | Values | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| `task_id` | Integer | 1–13 | Task number within the block (presentation order). Task 13 is always the dominated attention-check task. |
| `choice` | Integer | 1–4 | Chosen alternative: `1` = Train, `2` = Coach bus, `3` = Rural bus (3-mode designs only), `4` = Opt-out (none). |

**Question shown:** "If these were the only options for your habitual trip, which would you choose?"

**Availability:**

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| `av_train` | Always `1`. Train available in all OD pairs. |
| `av_bus` | Always `1`. Coach bus available in all OD pairs. |
| `av_micro` | `1` only in designs `Buin-BuinZoo-Linderos` and `Paine`; `0` otherwise. |

### 8.2 Quantitative attributes

For each mode prefix (`train`, `bus`, `micro`):

| Suffix | Unit | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `*_tt` | Minutes | In-vehicle travel time. |
| `*_cost` | CLP | Ticket cost, standard fare. |
| `*_cost_reduced` | CLP | Ticket cost, reduced fare. |
| `*_headway` | Minutes | Time between consecutive departures (service frequency). |
| `*_arrival_time_variability` | Minutes | Maximum possible departure delay. |
| `*_lmt` | Minutes | Last-mile access time displayed to the respondent. |

*Note on `*_lmt`:* the last-mile times shown to each participant depended on the last-mile mode the participant declared before the SP tasks (`last_mile_mode`). The design contained mode-of-access-specific last-mile time levels (walking, public transport, car/taxi), and `*_lmt` holds the value that was actually displayed to the respondent.

### 8.3 Qualitative attributes

| Field | Scale | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `train_crowding` | 1–5 | Train crowding level. `1` = Less than half of seats occupied, nobody standing. `2` = More than half occupied, nobody standing. `3` = All seats occupied, few people standing. `4` = All occupied, slight difficulty moving. `5` = All occupied, maximum difficulty moving. |
| `bus_crowding` | 1–3 | Coach bus crowding (all-seated service). `1` = Less than half of seats occupied. `2` = More than half. `3` = All seats occupied. |
| `micro_crowding` | 1–5 | Rural bus crowding. Same scale as the train. |
| `train_aircon` | 0–1 | `0` = No air conditioning, `1` = Air conditioning. |
| `bus_aircon` | 0–1 | Same, coach bus. |
| `micro_aircon` | 0–1 | Same, rural bus. |
| `train_vendors` | 0–1 | `0` = No street vendors/musicians on board, `1` = Usually present. |
| `bus_vendors` | 0–1 | Same, coach bus. Constant at `0` by design (not applicable to long-distance coaches). |
| `micro_vendors` | 0–1 | Same, rural bus. |

## 10. Choice Task Presentation (Screenshots)

*Examples of the SP choice tasks as displayed to respondents can be found in the 'docs/figures' folder. The survey instrument was administered in Spanish; the screenshots included in the `example-sp-2mode.png` and `example-sp-3mode.png` are reproductions of the original Qualtrics interface, for designs with two and three modes correspondingly.*

*Presentation notes:* the visual order of attribute rows in the SP table was randomized per respondent via JavaScript in Qualtrics to reduce order bias; travel costs displayed were automatically adjusted for respondents with reduced-fare benefits; and last-mile access times were pivoted based on the respondent's reported last-mile mode.
